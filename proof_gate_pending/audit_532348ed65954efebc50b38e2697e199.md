## Why Existing Checks Fail

The only guard on `_totalAmountForInterval` is the cap at `totalETHBurn`:

```solidity
if (_totalAmountForInterval > totalETHBurn) {
    _totalAmountForInterval = uint128(totalETHBurn);
}
```

This cap is a safety measure against overflow/over-allocation, but it has the side effect of setting the single-call allocation to the **entire pool** when enough intervals are missed. The incentive is then computed as `1.5% of totalETHBurn` rather than `1.5% of one interval's allocation`. There is no separate cap on the incentive amount itself, and no check that limits the incentive to a fraction of the daily allocation.

The `IntervalAlreadyBurned` guard (`if (currInterval.amountBurned != 0) revert`) only prevents replay within the same interval — it does not prevent the cap from being reached in the first place.

## Rejection Checks

**expected behavior checked:** The design intent is that missed intervals accumulate to incentivize callers. However, the incentive being 1.5% of the *entire pool* (vs. 1.5% of a single interval's ~0.035% of pool) is a 43× amplification that violates the per-call incentive bound. This is not documented as intended behavior.

**prior report checked:** No evidence of prior report in the indexed codebase.

**README/NatSpec checked:** No NatSpec on `_calculateIntervalsETHBurn` or `swapETHForTorusAndBurn` documents the cap-triggered full-pool incentive as expected.

**unsupported assumption checked:** The precondition (20 days of inactivity) is realistic — the live context shows `lastBurnedETHIntervalStartTimestamp = 1784021400` with only ~14.5 hours elapsed, meaning the contract is currently active, but a future period of inactivity is plausible.

**Impact correction vs. claim:** The claim states "~99 ETH worth" but this confuses `ethUsedForBurns` (historical total, ~99.4 ETH) with `totalETHBurn` (current pool balance, ~2.22 ETH per live context). The actual extractable incentive at current state is `2.22 ETH * 1.5% ≈ 0.033 ETH`. The mechanism is valid but the magnitude is smaller than claimed.
